# IT Service Management — ISO/IEC 20000-1 and ITIL 4

**Canonical sources:** ISO/IEC 20000-1:2018 (`spec:iso-iec-20000-1`); ITIL 4
(`spec:itil-4`).
**Role:** the ITSM domain — incident, change, problem and service-level
management.

## What they define
ISO/IEC 20000-1 specifies requirements for a Service Management System (SMS):
service planning, the change/incident/problem/request practices, and continual
improvement. ITIL 4 is the practice framework (34 management practices)
organizations use to operate the SMS — value streams, the service value chain,
and the guiding principles.

## Role in the crosswalk
For a governed AI system, ITSM is where operational accountability lives: an
incident has an owner and a timeline, a change is enabled through a controlled
path, a problem drives root-cause fixes. This is the operational home for
"governance & accountability" and "monitoring & post-market".

## Caveat
ITIL is guidance, not a certifiable standard (ISO/IEC 20000-1 is the certifiable
one). Adopting the vocabulary does not make the practice effective; the evidence
is in the incident/change records, not the process document.
