# NIST SP 800-53 — Security and Privacy Controls

**Canonical source:** NIST Special Publication 800-53 Rev. 5
(`doi:10.6028/NIST.SP.800-53r5`).
**Role:** the control catalog under secrets, resilience and PII.

## What it defines
A comprehensive catalog of security and privacy controls organized in families
(Access Control, Audit and Accountability, Contingency Planning, Identification
and Authentication, Incident Response, System and Communications Protection,
…), with control enhancements and baselines. It is the reference many US and
enterprise programs select and tailor from.

## Role in the crosswalk
It is the deep control reference that the operational domains draw on: key
management (SC family), backup and DR (CP family), audit (AU family), and
privacy controls. Where ISO 27001 Annex A is the umbrella, 800-53 is the
detailed menu.

## Caveat
800-53 is a catalog to tailor via baselines, not an all-or-nothing mandate; a
control being listed is not evidence it is implemented. Overlaps with ISO 27001
Annex A are mapped but not identical.
